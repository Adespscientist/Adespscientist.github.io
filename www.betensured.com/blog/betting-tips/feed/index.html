How To Place Winning Odds

Betting odds represent the probability of an event occuring, including the proposed return you as a bettor should receive on a winning bet.

Betting odds also indicate how likely your prediction is, and are represented as decimal, fractional, or American odds.

When it comes to odds, the highest potential return is important, because it endorses the accuracy of your bookmakers odds, and helps you choose the right bookmaker for yourself.

Odds can be confusing and if misunderstood can cost you a lot of money, so we will show you everything you need to know about How To Place Winning Odds so you don’t lose more money.

This section covers the types of betting odds, how betting odds work, how betting odds are read, how to calculate your winnings with odds, how bookmakers set football odds and live betting and changing odds.

Types Of Betting Odds

  • Decimal odds
  • Fractional Odds
  • American Odds

Decimal odds are more common in Nigeria than fractional and American odds, and as such, receive the most focus. American odds are more common in the United States Of America.

How  Betting Odds Work

When choosing the right odds for you, there are 3 factors you should consider. These 3 factors are the risk level,  the quality of odds, and comparing odds.

Risk Level

You need to choose your preferred risk level. 

Preference for risk level reads as, if you would like a higher chance of getting a wager return for a smaller amount, or a lower chance of getting a wager return but for a higher amount

Quality Of Odds

Vetting the quality of odds is how you make an analysis of betting markets.

Judging the quality of odds on offer is simply what features separate a well-thought out bet, from the one you recklessly placed.

Generally judging the quality of odds, you look at whether they are of good value, based on how the odds stacked up against your prediction of an outcome or your bookmakers prediction.

Comparing  Odds

Odds can be compared against their competition so that you can get a high return.

This is done by mathematically determining which of the compared odds can give you the highest betting returns.

How  Betting Odds Are Read

To be able to read betting odds, you should know that betting odds are represented as decimal, fractional and American odds. Each betting odd is read in a different way.

Decimal Odds

Decimal odds are easy to read and understand, because the higher your number of odds, the higher your winnings.

  • To calculate your decimal odds, your winnings are your stake, multiplied by the decimal odds.
  • In decimal odds 2.0 is even money, meaning your winnings will be double or x2, and anything less than is an odds-on bet.
  • Decimal odds are popular in Nigeria, because they are easy and used by Nigerian betting sites.

American Odds

American odds are used in America, and are more difficult to understand.

American odds use the plus and minus symbol, and number after each show how likely it is for your favourite team  to win or lose.

The higher the number after the minus sign,  the more likely it is for a team to win, but the higher the number after a plus, the higher the team’s chances of losing.

Fractional Odds

Just like American odds, fractional odds aren’t specific to Nigeria, rather they are mainly used in the UK. Fractional odds are used in street betting shops and on horse racing.

In fractional odds, the higher the number of odds, the higher you earn from your bet. For fractional odds the first half represents how much you will win, and second half represents how much you stake.

How To Calculate Your Winnings With Different Betting Odds

Here is a summary of how to calculate your winnings with the different betting odds.

Calculating Winnings With Decimal Odds

To calculate your winnings with decimal odds, multiply your stake by the decimal odds.

Example:

  • Stake – ₦10
  • Odds – 2.5
  • Winnings = ₦25

Calculating Winnings With Fractional Odds

To calculate your winnings with fractional odds, how much you will win/how much you stake

Example:

  • For a 5/1 odds, you would receive ₦5 for every ₦1 you bet.
  • If you are staking a different amount from the given fraction, maybe a ₦3 stake would be 3 x 5, earning you a ₦15.

Calculating Winnings With American Odds

American odds are usually split into two possibilities, minus (-) and plus (+) money lines.

The minus money line is how much you need to bet to win an amount of money while the plus money line is how much you would win if you bet an amount of money.

A N100 bet on a +200 moneyline, would win you ₦200, and a -200 money line requires you to bet  ₦200 to receive N100

How Bookmakers Set  Football Odds

Your bookmaker isn’t using magic to set the odds for that football game you bet on, here is how bookmakers set football odds.

Different Predictions

Bookmakers judge the likelihood of an event to occur differently. Each bookmaker based on their predictions, comes up with different conclusions for an odd.

Bookmakers are competitive, so the gap in differences between odds, wouldn’t be too wide amongst bookies.

Unforeseen Circumstances

A bookmaker’s odds can be affected by unforeseen circumstances like player injuries, or team lineup which impacted the game, and in turn affected the bookie’s odds.

Match Timings

Your bookmaker’s odds can be affected by impromptu match timings as well. If any games happen between the team you wish to bet on and another team within the time of the odds being initially set and the event itself, and the team you bet on plays badly against another team, their odds will lengthen because it’s a negative sign for their future performance in a match with the team they were initially supposed to play.

The Team’s History

The history between the two teams playing a match can also affect a bookie’s odds, as well as other factors like recent form, injury news, and the environment.

Amount Of Money

Sometimes, a bookmaker’s odds can also be based on the amount of money bookies discover on one side of a wager.

This is because Bookmakers all want to accept your bet, but they are also weighing so many factors to ensure they don’t lose much money to an incorrect prediction.

Bookmakers may have varying odds,  but it’s just based on different opinions or their reaction to the market and this shouldn’t be taken in as a quality odd. To ensure that your bookie’s odds are high quality, you need to consider their over-round and bookmaker’s margin.

Live Betting And Changing Odds

Bettors need to understand and accept that sometimes, odds for live betting can change.

However, the  changes in odds that occur during a live betting are the events of the match which you couldn’t have predicted before the game started.

It’s common for changes in odds to occur in live betting as live betting possesses a bigger challenge than pre-match because it is fast-paced and has a lot  to take into account.
